# Content/Reference Lens — Scoring Rubrics

Business model: Content platforms, reference sites, educational resources, comparison guides, newsletters, and media properties. Value comes from curated, organized, or original content rather than listings or transactions.

Revenue models: Display ads (RPM-based), newsletter sponsorships, premium content/paywalls, affiliate links within content, courses/ebooks, consulting leads.

## Weight Overrides

Content business success is overwhelmingly driven by distribution (SEO/newsletter growth). Content scoring research shows conversion metrics account for 45% of value, engagement 35%, and SEO performance 20%. Automation is weighted higher because AI content generation is a game-changer for content businesses — but quality still determines ranking and audience retention.

## Key Benchmarks (from research)
- RPM by vertical: Finance $15-50, Tech $10-25, Lifestyle $5-15, Greek market $0.75-2
- Domain Authority 40-60 = well-established site
- Newsletter economics: 10K subscribers at $50 CPM sponsorship = $500/issue
- Content scoring weights: conversion 45%, engagement 35%, SEO 20%
- Ad network progression: AdSense (any) → Ezoic (10K) → Mediavine (25K) → Raptive (100K)

## Lens-Specific Evidence Requirements

### Criterion 3: Revenue Potential
- Score >=4: High-RPM vertical (finance, insurance, legal = $15-50 RPM) OR premium content model viable ($10+/mo)
- Score >=3: Moderate RPM vertical ($5-15) with 50K+ monthly visitor potential
- Key signals: Display ad RPMs for the vertical, newsletter sponsorship rates, affiliate program commissions
- Revenue benchmarks: 100K monthly pageviews at $10 RPM = $1K/mo; newsletter with 10K subscribers at $50 CPM sponsorship = $500/issue

### Criterion 4: Competitor Revenue Validation
- Score >=4: Content competitors with visible monetization (Mediavine/Raptive ads, paid newsletters, courses)
- Score >=3: Competitors with Tranco rank <100K and 2+ years of operation
- Key signals: Ad networks visible (Mediavine = 25K+ monthly sessions), Substack subscriber counts, course platforms (Teachable, Gumroad)

### Criterion 7: Automation Potential
- Focus on: Content creation and curation automation
- Score >=4: Programmatic content generation from structured data (each data point = a page)
- Score >=3: AI-assisted content creation with human editing
- Score <=2: All content requires expert-level original writing/research
- Unique to content: AI content generation can bootstrap but risks Google penalties; human expertise adds defensibility

### Criterion 8: Distribution Opportunity
- Focus on: SEO (primary), newsletter growth, social sharing, community
- Score >=4: Hundreds of long-tail keywords with low competition, strong social sharing potential
- Score >=3: 50+ rankable keywords, newsletter topic with proven audience
- Key signals: Google Suggest depth, "People Also Ask" volume, Reddit/HN engagement on topic

### Criterion 9: Defensibility
- Primary moats: Expertise/authority, brand recognition, audience/email list, content depth, community
- Score >=4: Unique expertise or data that cannot be replicated + engaged community
- Score >=3: First-mover content advantage with growing audience
- Score <=2: Generic content that AI can replicate; no unique voice or data

### Criterion 11: Founder-Team Fit
- Weight: 9% (expanded — domain expertise is the primary defensibility driver for content)
- Ask: (1) Does the founder have genuine expertise or unique POV in this content niche? (2) Do they have an existing audience (newsletter, social) they can distribute to immediately? (3) Can they produce content at the required cadence solo or with a small team? (4) Do they understand SEO well enough to target the right keywords?
- Score >=4: Founder is a recognized expert or practitioner in the niche + has existing audience + SEO competency
- Score <=2: No domain expertise, no audience, content would be generic and undifferentiated

### Criterion 12: MVP Speed
- Score >=4: 10-20 articles + SEO optimization = launch in 1-2 weeks
- Score >=3: 30-50 pieces of content with basic site = 3-4 weeks
- Score <=2: Requires deep research, expert interviews, or original data collection = 2+ months

### Criterion 13: Early Validation Signal
- Weight: 4% (lower weight — content can build audience over time post-launch without pre-launch signals)
- Score 4: Newsletter or social account already has 100+ subscribers/followers in this niche
- Score 3: Published 3-5 pieces of content on the topic with measurable engagement (shares, comments)
- Score <=2: No content published; no audience built yet
