# Draftloom — User Personas

## Persona 1: The Developer Blogger

**Name:** George · Senior frontend developer, 10+ years experience
**Blogs about:** AI tooling, frontend architecture, building in public
**Publishes on:** Personal site, cross-posts to X and LinkedIn
**Post frequency:** 2–4 per month
**Typical length:** Medium (800–1,200 words)

**Goals:**
- Write posts that sound like him, not like generic AI output
- Get traction on X without spending hours on hooks
- Rank for niche technical keywords without becoming an SEO robot

**Pain points:**
- Starts drafts in Notion, abandons half-finished
- Hook is always weak — spends the most time on the first sentence
- Voice drifts when AI helps — sounds too formal or too casual

**How Draftloom helps:**
- Voice profile captures his opinionated, technical tone
- Hook eval specifically scores and patches the first line
- Section wireframe removes the blank-page paralysis

**Profile example:** `george-personal` · audience: indie hackers + frontend devs · tone: direct, opinionated, technical

## Persona 2: The Content Marketer

**Name:** Sara · Head of Content at a B2B SaaS startup, 5 years in content
**Blogs about:** Product updates, industry thought leadership, SEO-driven comparisons
**Publishes on:** Company blog, Medium, LinkedIn
**Post frequency:** 6–8 per month across multiple formats
**Typical length:** Long (1,500–2,500 words)

**Goals:**
- Hit SEO targets without sacrificing readability
- Keep brand voice consistent across multiple contributors
- Produce distribution-ready copy alongside each post

**Pain points:**
- Brand voice is hard to maintain when multiple people write
- SEO checklists are tedious and easy to forget
- Repurposing content for LinkedIn/email takes hours after each post

**How Draftloom helps:**
- Multiple profiles (`brand-blog`, `thought-leadership`) maintain voice consistency
- SEO eval enforces keyword density, heading structure, and meta fields automatically
- Distribution agent generates LinkedIn opener and email subject immediately

**Profile example:** `vanguard-corporate` · audience: CTOs and engineering managers · tone: authoritative, practical, data-driven

## Persona 3: The Newsletter Writer

**Name:** Amara · Indie creator, 8,000 subscriber newsletter on AI tools
**Blogs about:** AI tool reviews, workflow tips, industry takes
**Publishes on:** Substack, cross-posts to X
**Post frequency:** Weekly (newsletter) + 2–3 social posts per week
**Typical length:** Short to medium (600–1,000 words)

**Goals:**
- Write fast — newsletter goes out every Tuesday
- Maintain a loyal readership who knows her voice
- Generate X threads and email subjects without extra work

**Pain points:**
- Speed pressure means quality suffers — hooks are rushed
- Readability drops under time pressure (walls of text)
- Distribution copy is always an afterthought

**How Draftloom helps:**
- `typical_length: short` in profile keeps drafts tight
- 1-pass draft for speed, eval loop optional when time allows
- Distribution agent runs automatically — X hook and email subject ready immediately

**Profile example:** `amara-newsletter` · audience: AI-curious professionals · tone: conversational, witty, opinionated

## Persona 4: The Agency Writer

**Name:** Luca · Freelance content writer managing 5–8 client accounts
**Blogs about:** Varies by client — fintech, climate tech, developer tools
**Publishes on:** Client blogs, Ghost, WordPress
**Post frequency:** 15–20 posts per month across clients
**Typical length:** Varies (800–2,000 words by client)

**Goals:**
- Switch voice between clients without mental overhead
- Deliver SEO-optimised drafts that clients approve faster
- Reduce revision rounds

**Pain points:**
- Forgetting one client's tone when switching context
- Client feedback is usually "it doesn't sound like us"
- SEO requirements differ by client and are hard to track

**How Draftloom helps:**
- One profile per client (`client-fintech`, `client-climatetech`, `client-devtools`)
- Voice eval catches tone drift before client sees the draft
- SEO eval per post catches missing optimisations before submission

**Profile example:** Multiple profiles, one per client · each with different audience, tone, pillars, and SEO keyword defaults
